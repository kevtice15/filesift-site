Features

A closer look at FileSift.

Everything FileSift does to help you clear a folder fast — moving files into folders, or tagging them in place.

The core idea

Two ways to sort

Move files into folders, tag them where they are, or do both in a single pass.

Move into folders

Send each file to the right place — Keep, Delete, a project folder, wherever. Turn a camera dump or Downloads pile into a tidy folder structure.

Tag in place

Leave files where they are and apply a macOS Finder tag. Categorize and color-code your files where they live, so they stay findable without rearranging a thing.

Two views

The right view for the job

Work one file at a time, or scan a whole folder at once. Switch whenever you like.

Focus

One image or video at a time in a large preview, with a filmstrip of what's next. For when detail matters before you decide.

Grid

Your whole folder as a contact sheet. Resize the thumbnails, and fit or fill the frame to see more at a glance.

One folder, both views

Flip between Focus and Grid anytime. The same folder, destinations, and staged plan carry across — it's a density choice, not two apps.

Speed

Clear a backlog quickly

Assign with a keystroke and keep moving. FileSift jumps to the next file for you.

Sort with number keys

Press 1 through 9 to send a file to a destination, in both views. The next file comes up automatically, so you can clear a whole pile at a rhythm.

Grab many at once

In Grid, select a whole group — drag a box around them, shift-click a range, or move with the arrow keys — then file them in one move.

Stage, or go instant

By default, assignments stack up so you can review before anything moves. Prefer no waiting? Turn on instant mode to file each one on the spot.

Auto-sort

Sort it automatically

Auto-sort organizes a folder by type, date, month, size, orientation, or extension. FileSift sorts the files and builds the folders; nothing moves until you review.

By type

Images, videos, documents, audio, archives, code, and more.

By extension

One group per file extension, for finer separation.

By date

Group by year or month, using a photo's capture date when it has one.

By size

Separate the big files from the small ones.

By orientation

Portrait, landscape, and square, sorted apart.

Destinations

Set up where things go

Build your set of destinations once, and reuse it on the next folder.

Add or drag in folders

Create a destination in the app, or drag folders straight from Finder — several at once if you like.

Suggested folders

FileSift spots the subfolders already inside your source and offers them as destinations, so existing structure is one click to reuse.

Presets

Save a set of destinations as a preset and apply it to the next folder, so a routine cleanup starts ready to go.

See clearly

Know what you're filing

Enough detail to decide, without leaving the app.

Quick Look

Press Space to preview any file full-size, the same way Finder does.

Play video in Focus

Watch a clip right in the preview with native controls; volume carries to the next one.

Details panel

Name, size, dimensions, and date for the file in front of you.

Filter and track progress

Narrow to All, Unassigned, or Assigned, and watch a progress bar follow the session.

Whatever's in there

Handles your whole folder

Photos and video, or every file type — sorted safely.

Every file type

Images and video by default, or switch on all file types to sort PDFs, documents, JSON, and anything else on your Mac.

Works with network drives

Sorting from a NAS or file share? Choose to move files, or copy them and keep the original where it is.

Safe by default

Nothing moves until you press Sort Now, same-named files get a unique name instead of overwriting, and every step can be undone.

On your Mac

Private and native

A real Mac app that keeps your files to itself.

Everything on-device

No account, no uploads, no tracking. Your files never leave your Mac.

A real Mac app

Light and dark mode, your system accent color, full keyboard control, and Increase Contrast support.

Honest bug reports

Reporting a problem includes only non-identifying diagnostics, and nothing is sent until you review and approve it.

Resize the grid

Scale the thumbnails up or down, and fit or fill the frame.

Folders anywhere

Sort a folder anywhere on your Mac, with destinations inside or outside it.

Undo any step

Change your mind on an assignment and take it back with one shortcut.

Keyboard cheat sheet

Forget a shortcut? A full reference is a keypress away inside the app.

Settings that fit your setup

Tune behavior like move-vs-copy for network volumes from a standard Settings window.
