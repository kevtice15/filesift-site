Two ways to sort

Move it, or tag it.

Organize files however suits the job — relocate them into folders, or label them where they sit.

Move into folders

Send each file to the right place — Keep, Delete, a project folder, wherever. The fastest way to turn a camera dump or Downloads pile into a tidy folder structure.

Tag in place

Don't want to move anything? Apply a macOS Finder tag instead. Categorize and color-code your files where they already live, so they stay findable without rearranging a thing.

How it works

1

Open a folder

Point FileSift at the folder you want to clean up.

2

Sort it, fast

Auto-sort by type, date, size, and more — or do it by hand in Grid (many at once) or Focus (one up close). Move files into folders, or tag them in place.

3

Review, then Sort Now

Nothing has moved yet. Check the plan, press Sort Now, and FileSift files everything at once.

Sort a whole folder at once

Grid lays your files out as a contact sheet. Grab a bunch at a time — drag a box around them, shift-click a range, or move with the arrow keys — then send the whole group to a folder or tag in one move.

Nothing moves until you review and confirm.

Get a closer look with Focus

When detail matters, Focus shows one image or video at a time in a large preview — so you can really see what you're deciding on before you file it.

Sort it automatically

Auto-sort organizes a folder by type, date, month, size, orientation, or extension — FileSift sorts the files and builds the folders for you. Review the result, then Sort Now.

Sort with the number keys

In both Grid and Focus, press 1 through 9 to send a file to a destination — FileSift jumps to the next one automatically, so you can clear a whole backlog at speed.

Everything happens on your Mac

No account, no uploads, no tracking. FileSift sorts your files locally — they never leave your device. Read the privacy policy

Questions

Does it move or copy my files?

It moves them into the folders you choose. If you'd rather not move anything, use tags instead — your files stay exactly where they are and just get a macOS Finder tag.

What kinds of files can it sort?

Images and videos out of the box, with the option to include every other file type too — PDFs, documents, and anything else on your Mac.

What if I change my mind?

Nothing is moved until you confirm, and you can undo. You stay in control the whole way through.

What do I need to run it?

A Mac running macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later.

Found a bug, or want to get in touch?

Use Report a Bug in the app (in the sidebar and the Help menu), or email filesiftapp@fastmail.com. A report includes only non-identifying diagnostics — app version, basic state, and the app's recent log — never your file names or a screenshot, and nothing is sent until you review and approve it.
